Lembas Bread: High energy, low maintenance.

Description

Lembas bread, also known as waybread, is a type of bread featured in J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth legendarium. It is a staple food for the Elves and is said to have nourishing and sustaining properties, allowing those who eat it to travel long distances without feeling hunger or fatigue.

According to the stories, lembas bread is made from the flour of the mallorn tree, mixed with honey and the elixir of life. It is baked in a special way that preserves its freshness and flavor for a long time, even without the use of preservatives. The bread is also wrapped in leaves to protect it and keep it moist.

In the stories, lembas bread is often given as a gift and is considered a symbol of friendship and hospitality. It is also considered a sacred food, and it is believed that those who eat it are able to gain insight and wisdom.

Ingredients

  • 6 TBSP butter or margarine, slightly softened
  • 2 cups self-rising flour
  • 1 TBSP granulated sugar
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)
  • 1 egg, well beaten
  • ½ cup milk
  • 4 TBSP heavy cream
  • Mallorn leaves

Steps

  1. With a pastry blender of fork, cut margarine into the flour in a mixing bowl until the mixture resembles cornmeal
  2. Add the sugar and if desired, ½ cup of raisins
  3. In a small bowl, beat the egg and milk together until mixed. Reserve 1 TBSP of this mixture to brush the tops of the Lembas
  4. Knead three or four times on a lightly floured surface. Roll dough to a ¾" thickness and cut with an oval or leaf shaped cookie cutter
  5. Brush the tops of the Lembas with the reserved egg-milk mixture. Bake for 12 -13 minutes in a preheated 400 degree oven
